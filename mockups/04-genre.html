About
Wikipedia
Wikipedia — Post-rock

Post-rock is a type of experimental rock music that uses traditional rock instruments to achieve non-traditional sonic results. The term was coined by music critic Simon Reynolds in a 1994 article for Mojo magazine, in which he used it to describe Bark Psychosis's album Hex.

Post-rock bands typically employ electric guitar, bass, and drums in ways that diverge from traditional rock song structures — avoiding verse-chorus forms in favour of long, instrumental compositions that evolve gradually through texture, dynamics, and atmosphere. Lyrics are absent or minimal. The influence of ambient, jazz, electronic, and classical music is pronounced.

Key early acts include Talk Talk, Slint, Bark Psychosis, and Tortoise. The genre flourished in the late 1990s and early 2000s with groups like Godspeed You! Black Emperor, Explosions in the Sky, and Mogwai.
